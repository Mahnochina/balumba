VAZ classic

VAZ trademark of the family of cars of the Volga Automobile Plant, assigned to the VAZ-2101 model in June 1970. The first Zhiguli model was based on a modified Italian Fiat 124 model.[1] Cars of the VAZ-2101/2102/2103 family were sold under the trade name "Zhiguli" on the domestic and international markets (except for several European countries, where the word "Zhiguli" was associated by buyers with the dubious word gigolo, as well as in Hungary, where it generally coincided with the obscene in a word). Therefore, on the foreign market, the Zhiguli trademark was quickly replaced by LADA.

VAZ-2101

VAZ-2101 "Zhiguly" is a small Soviet rear-wheel drive passenger car with a sedan-type body. The first model produced by the Volga Automobile Plant. The so-called "classic" family of VAZ cars was created on the basis of the VAZ-2101, which was on the assembly line until September 17, 2012.
